Every early warning vendor quotes recall. Ask for the rest of the matrix.

The digital dollar layer

Everyone rates the stablecoin. Nobody scores the banks holding its cash.

The fast layer

A bank that suddenly pays up for deposits is telling you something. We listen weekly.

Reaching for deposits is one of the oldest tells in banking and one of the best documented in the academic record. As a quarterly signal from filings alone it catches 64.7 percent of all 550 failures by itself, and adding it lifts the full engine from 72.0 to 75.5 percent recall at the same alarm budget. The live layer sharpens it to weeks: every bank's offered rates against the FDIC national baseline, where a legal ceiling already exists for banks that are less than well capitalized.

Deposit pricing vendors sell this data to banks to help them price. Nobody sells it as a distress signal, because their customers are the banks. Ours are not.

Method and limits

Read this before relying on any of it.

POINT IN TIME

No hindsight anywhere

A score at any date uses only filings available at that date. Signal weights were fixed before the first backtest ran and never tuned on outcomes. Post hoc additions, private credit exposure and deposit desperation, are validated standalone and reported as separate score versions.

FRAUD BLINDNESS

We do not detect crime

Every 2023 to 2026 miss was a fraud driven failure. Balance sheet fundamentals cannot see embezzlement, and we will not pretend otherwise. A conduct and enforcement signal is a roadmap item, not a tuning fix.

PROXY, NOT TAPE

Regulators see more

The Fed watches actual payment flows in real time. Nobody outside can. Public data nowcasting approximates run pressure rather than observing it, and the honest word for that is proxy.

COVERAGE

Known data gaps, disclosed

Uninsured deposit estimates have a reporting gap for smaller banks between 2015 and 2019. The score reweights over available signals and records how many were present for every bank quarter.

LiquiLens US outputs are screening percentiles computed from public FDIC filings. They are research, not credit ratings, not a prediction that any institution will fail, and not investment, legal or deposit placement advice. Named institutions are identified because their filings are public records; nothing on this page alleges wrongdoing by anyone. Data: FDIC BankFind Suite, public domain.
