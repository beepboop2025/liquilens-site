The gap

We opened equities. We are halfway through bonds. Nobody opened the money market.

A bank parks its spare rupee overnight and earns close to seven percent. A corporate treasury does the same. Their cash never sleeps. A small business keeps its working capital in a current account earning zero, while the same rupee, in the same hours, could be working. That is not a pricing problem. It is an access problem, and it sits underneath the whole of Indian finance.

    Questions

    Plain answers.

    What is LiquiLens?

    LiquiLens is an autonomous treasury and liquidity layer for Indian businesses and lenders. It watches the cash a business already holds, puts idle balances to work in the money market, the safest and most liquid corner of finance, and monitors a live lending book for liquidity risk. Six engines, each grounded in published research, do the watching.

    Who is it for?

    Two groups. Indian businesses sitting on idle cash that earns nothing overnight, and lenders, NBFCs in particular, who need to see liquidity stress in their book before it becomes a headline. Banks have had this machinery for decades. Everyone else has spreadsheets.

    Is LiquiLens only for India?

    India is the product market and the deepest build. The engine underneath is market agnostic, and it now runs a second live book: run risk scores for every FDIC insured US bank, validated against all 550 failures since 2008 with the full confusion matrix published. Both 2026 US failures were on that watchlist a year early, out of sample. The US layer lives here, and a stablecoin reserve layer rides on it.

    Why does the money market matter for an ordinary business?

    The money market is where banks and large corporates park cash overnight and borrow short term. It is the deepest, safest pool in Indian finance, and it has effectively been closed to everyone below a certain size. LiquiLens exists to open that access, so a mid-size company's cash can work through the night the way a bank's does.

    What is the RBI LCR change and why is there a countdown on this page?

    The RBI is extending Liquidity Coverage Ratio style rules deeper into the NBFC sector, with thresholds that start applying as balance sheets cross 5,000 crore rupees. The calculator on this page estimates the month your growth curve crosses that line. If you are a lender, that date is when liquidity risk stops being optional to measure.

    How big is the gap LiquiLens is built for?

    Large, and documented. India's MSME credit gap was estimated at about ₹25 lakh crore as of March 2025, with only 14% of MSMEs holding formal credit (Deloitte, 2025). MSMEs contribute roughly 30% of India's GDP and about 45% of exports (Ministry of MSME, 2024). On the cash side, RBI directions prohibit banks from paying interest on current accounts, so idle working capital earns zero by rule, not by accident.

    Is LiquiLens live? How do I get access?

    Yes. The platform runs in production. The live walkthrough at demo.liquilens.in is open by invitation while we work with early design partners: request an access code there and we open it for you. Its Evidence tab replays the crisis validation record on real public filings, institution by institution, with every source cited and every miss shown; the treasury console around it runs on synthetic data, so nothing confidential is inside. Pilot tenants run isolated on the same infrastructure. If you run treasury at a business or risk at a lender, email the founder or connect on LinkedIn below. Design partners shape what gets built next.

    Has any of this been tested on real data?

    Yes, twice over, on the public record. We replayed the balance sheet engine on 36 institutions of the 2018 to 2021 crisis, failed lenders, stressed survivors and healthy controls together: 9 of 10 failures flagged ahead of the record excluding books concealed by fraud, at a median lead of 15 months, with a 1 in 10 false alarm rate on healthy controls and significance at p < 0.001. Then we ran a fully out of sample replay on the 2025 microfinance cycle: 11 more institutions, zero false alarms on 8 controls, and one lender flagged below investment grade a quarter before the rating agencies moved. The earlier network signal study on rating timelines flagged 7 of 8 defaults of 2018 and 2019 at a median of 6.5 months, with Yes Bank at 17 months in the pre stated confirmatory phase. These are studies on public data, not a validation on a live book. The next number comes from a lender backtest run on the lender's own servers, and that is exactly what we offer design partners.

    What is Seiche, and why does a treasury company run a free terminal?

    Seiche is our free, open source terminal for funding stress in the US money market. It runs on public data, publishes its forecasts before the outcome, and keeps a sealed scoreboard of hits and misses that anyone can audit at seiche.info. We run it because an early warning vendor should have a public track record, and because the research that watches the world's deepest money market is the same research that watches an Indian lender's book. Seiche stays free forever. It is the record; LiquiLens is the product.

    What is the MFI Risk Board on this page?

    It is the production system's live scoring board for India's registered microfinance lenders, rendered on this page straight from the same API the product runs on. The RBI's official register lists over 9,000 NBFCs, 95 of them NBFC MFIs. Every MFI with a vetted dossier of primary filings gets scored by the same ensemble that was validated on two crisis cycles, and the trajectory next to each name is its score drawn quarter by quarter. Scores are disclosure based reads of public filings, not credit ratings. Coverage is published, exclusions carry a stated reason, and a row is dropped when its filings go stale.

    What happens to our data? How is it protected?

    Every lender's book is isolated to that lender. The regulatory tables, CRR, SLR, ALM and reports, are scoped per tenant and the isolation is enforced by automated tests on every commit, so a request for another tenant's data returns nothing at all. Access runs on short lived tokens with rotation and role based permissions, HTTPS is enforced everywhere, dependencies and secrets are scanned continuously, and a hash chained ledger records every alert as it was published so the history cannot be quietly rewritten. For pilots that cannot let data leave the building, the entire platform runs on the lender's own servers.
